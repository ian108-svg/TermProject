Explore the Types of Pans

Cast Iron

Durable and versatile, cast iron pans retain heat well, making them perfect for searing and slow-cooking.

Aluminum

Lightweight and affordable, aluminum pans are excellent for quick heating and even cooking.

Carbon Steel

Similar to cast iron but lighter, carbon steel pans heat up quickly and are easy to maneuver.

Ceramic

Non-toxic and easy to clean, ceramic pans are great for low-heat cooking and delicate foods.

Stainless Steel

Highly durable and resistant to rust, stainless steel pans offer a great balance of performance and longevity.

Non-Stick

Ideal for cooking delicate foods with less oil, non-stick pans are easy to use and clean.

Hard-Anodized

Known for durability and resistance to scratches, hard-anodized pans distribute heat evenly and are easy to clean.
